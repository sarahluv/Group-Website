Basketball World








Basketball was invented in 1891 in Springfeild Massachusetts, by James Naismith. Basketball was originally played with peach baskets and a soccer styled ball. Every time a point was scored, the game was paused so the janitor could bring out a ladder and retrieve the ball.


Original Rules


1. The ball is allowed to be thrown in any direction with one or both hands.
2. The ball can be batted in any direction with one or both hands.
3. Players can not run with the ball, the player mus throw the ball from the spot where he catches it.
4. The ball has to be held in or between the hands. The arms or body can not be used to hold the ball.
5. No shouldering,holding,pushing,tripping or striking in any way is allowed. If any of these are done this will result in a foul the first time. If it is done again will disqualify the player until the next goal is made or if the player is injured it will be for the whole game.
6. A foul is striking the ball with the fist,and violations of rules 3 and 4.
7. If either team makes three fouls in a row then it will count as a goal for the other team.
8. A goal is made when the ball is thrown or batted from the ground into the basket and stays there.
9. When the ball goes out of bounds it should be thrown in and played by the first person touching it.The person throwing it in has 5 seconds to throw it in. If the ball is held longer it goes to the opponent.
10. The umpire is the judge of the players and determines the fouls and notify the refferee when three fouls have been made in a row.
11. The referee is the judge of the ball and decides when the ball is in play, in-bounds,to which side it belongs to and keeps time. Decides when a goal has been made keeps count of the goals.
12. There are 15 minute halves, with five minute breakes in between.
13. The side that makes the most goals in the time being played is declared the winner. If the game is tied at the end the games can be played until another goal is made.

The First Game

After the 13 rules were published Naismith, who was a physical education instructor, asked his class to play a match in the Armory street court. The game was played 9 versus 9 with a soccer ball and peach baskets.
